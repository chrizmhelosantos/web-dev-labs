Interview Prep

Storytelling, practice, and presence

Preparing for interviews is as much about mindset as content. Start by choosing 4–6 stories that demonstrate leadership, problem-solving, collaboration, and learning. Use the STAR framework (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to make answers concise and outcome-focused. For technical roles, code and whiteboard practice is important — simulate real constraints, time yourself, and explain your thinking out loud. For behavioral interviews, rehearse answers but avoid sounding robotic: record yourself and iterate and practice with a friend or mirror.

Logistics matter: confirm time zones, test your tech (camera, mic, internet speed), pick a neutral background, and have a simple portfolio or sample ready to share. Before the interview, do a brief breathing exercise and revisit your top stories — 3 slow breaths resets your nervous system. Ask thoughtful questions at the end — about team culture, success metrics, or onboarding. These show curiosity and help you evaluate fit.

Quick practice checklist

  • Pick 4–6 STAR stories and rehearse out loud.
  • Mock interview for 20–30 minutes with timed answers.
  • Prepare 3 smart questions for the interviewer.
  • Confirm logistics 24 hours before and 30 minutes before.
